Complaint Guidelines & Required Documents

Everything you need to know before filing a land-related complaint.

Home Guidelines

Please read all guidelines carefully before submitting your complaint to ensure faster processing.

How to File a Complaint

  • 1

    Gather Your Documents

    Before starting, make sure you have your National ID, UPI (parcel number), and any supporting evidence ready.

  • 2

    Fill in Your Personal Details (Step 1)

    Provide your full name, gender, phone number, email, National ID, and residence information accurately.

  • 3

    Enter Land Information (Step 2)

    Select the province and district of the land in question, then provide the sector, cell, and UPI (parcel number).

  • 4

    Describe Your Complaint (Step 3)

    Choose the complaint category and write a clear, detailed description of your issue. Mention dates, people involved, and any previous attempts to resolve it.

  • 5

    Upload Supporting Documents

    Attach any relevant files (photos, PDFs etc.) that support your complaint. Max size: 5MB per file.

  • 6

    Submit & Save Your Tracking Number

    After submitting, you will receive a unique tracking number. Keep it safe to monitor your complaint's progress.

Rules & Policies

  • One complaint per land parcel at a time You may not submit multiple overlapping complaints for the same parcel.
  • Truthful and accurate information Providing false information may result in your complaint being dismissed.
  • Processing time: 30 working days Complaints are reviewed within 30 working days from submission.
  • You will be notified of progress Updates will be sent via SMS or email to the contact you provided.

Required Documents

Prepare these documents before starting the form.

  • National ID or Passport Required A valid government-issued ID for identity verification.
  • Land UPI (Parcel Number) Required The Unique Parcel Identifier of the land in question (format: 1/03/08/04/XXXX).
  • Land Title Document Optional A scanned copy of your land title or ownership certificate, if available.
  • Photos / Evidence Optional Photos of the land or any physical evidence related to the dispute (JPG or PNG, max 5MB).
  • Previous Correspondence (if any) Optional Letters, receipts or decisions from other institutions you previously contacted about this matter.

Accepted formats: PDF, PNG, JPG. Maximum file size: 5MB.

Ready to file?

Once you've gathered all the required documents, go ahead and start your complaint.
