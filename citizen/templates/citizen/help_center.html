Frequently Asked Questions

What is LandConnect?

LandConnect is the official online platform of the Rwanda National Land Authority (RNLA) that allows citizens to file, track, and manage land-related complaints.

Who can file a complaint?

Any Rwandan citizen or resident who has a land-related grievance can submit a complaint. You do not need to create an account — simply provide your National ID and contact details.

How long does it take to process my complaint?

The standard processing time is 30 working days from the date of submission. Complex cases may take longer. You will be notified of any updates via SMS or email.

How do I track my complaint?

After submitting your complaint, you receive a unique tracking number (e.g. LC-2024-123456). Go to the Tracking page on our website and enter this number to see the current status.

What documents do I need to file a complaint?

Required: National ID and the Land UPI (parcel number). Optional but helpful: land title, photos of the land, previous correspondence with other institutions.

Can I submit a complaint without an account?

Yes. You can file a complaint as a guest by providing your contact information. Creating an account allows you to manage and view all your complaints from a personal dashboard.
